name: park2024
description: Generative agent simulations of 1,052 real individuals using 2-hour qualitative interviews; interview-based agents match human self-consistency rates on GSS (85%) and outperform demographic/persona baselines by 14-15 points
type: reference
---

**Claim** — Interview-based generative agents predict participants' GSS responses with a normalized accuracy of 0.85, meaning agents predict individuals as accurately as those individuals replicate their own answers two weeks later.
**Quote** — "The generative agents replicate participants' responses on the General Social Survey 85% as accurately as participants replicate their own answers two weeks later."
**Page** — 1 (abstract)

**Claim** — Interview-based agents outperform both demographic-based (normalized accuracy 0.71) and persona-based (0.70) agents by 14–15 normalized points on the GSS; the difference is highly significant.
**Quote** — "The demographic-based generative agents achieved a normalized accuracy of 0.71 (std = 0.11), while persona-based agents reached 0.70 (std = 0.11). An ANOVA of the accuracy rates rejected the null hypothesis of no significant difference (F(2, 3153) = 989.62, p < 0.001)."
**Page** — 6

**Claim** — Interview-based agents replicated the same four out of five social science experiments that human participants replicated, with predicted effect sizes highly correlated with observed human effect sizes (r = 0.98).
**Quote** — "The effect sizes estimated from the generative agents were highly correlated with those of the participants (r = 0.98), compared to the participants' internal consistency correlation of 0.99, yielding a normalized correlation of 0.99."
**Page** — 7

**Claim** — For Big Five personality traits, interview-based agents achieve a normalized correlation of 0.80, compared to 0.55 for demographic-based and 0.75 for persona-based agents.
**Quote** — "For the Big Five, the generative agents achieved a normalized correlation of 0.80 (std = 1.88)... the interview-based generative agents outperformed both demographic-based (normalized correlation = 0.55) and persona-based (normalized correlation = 0.75) agents."
**Page** — 6

**Claim** — Interview-based agents reduce accuracy disparities across racial and ideological subgroups. Political ideology DPD dropped from 12.35% (demographic-based) to 7.85% (interview-based); Big Five DPD dropped from 0.165 to 0.063.
**Quote** — "For political ideology, we observed that in the GSS, the DPD dropped from 12.35% for demographic-based generative agents to 7.85% for interview-based generative agents. In the Big Five personality traits, the DPD dropped from 0.165 to 0.063."
**Page** — 9

**Claim** — Even removing 80% of the interview transcript (96 out of 120 minutes), interview-based agents still outperform composite agents that were given direct access to near-equivalent survey instrument responses.
**Quote** — "Even when we randomly removed 80% of the interview transcript—equivalent to removing 96 minutes of the 120-minute interview—the interview-based generative agents still outperformed the composite agents, achieving an average normalized accuracy of 0.79 (std = 0.11) on the GSS."
**Page** — 7

**Claim** — Interview-based agents do not outperform baselines on behavioral economic games; there was no significant difference in MAE across agent types for economic games.
**Quote** — "However, there was no significant difference in MAE between the agents for the economic games (F(2, 3153) = 0.12, p = 0.89)."
**Page** — 6

**Claim** — The architecture uses the full 2-hour interview transcript injected into the model prompt; agents are not trained on the interview but query the transcript at inference time.
**Quote** — "When an agent is queried, the entire interview transcript is injected into the model prompt, instructing the model to imitate the person based on their interview data."
