# Conrad, Schober & Coiner (2007)

**Full citation:** Conrad, F. G., Schober, M. F., & Coiner, T. (2007). Bringing features of human dialogue to web surveys. *Applied Cognitive Psychology, 21*(2), 165–187.

## Study overview

- **Mode:** Web survey (two lab experiments)
- **Survey topic:** Questions from ongoing US government surveys — employment (CPS), housing (CPI-Housing), purchases (CPOPS)
- **Clarification type:** Respondent-initiated (click on highlighted term) and mixed-initiative (system also proactively offers clarification when respondent is slow to respond, i.e., inactivity threshold exceeded)
- **Scenario method:** Fictional scenarios with known correct answers, half straightforward and half complicated (ambiguous w.r.t. official definition)

## Experiment 1

- **N:** 54 respondents (lab, Washington DC area, paid)
- **Design:** 5 conditions — no clarification; user-initiated + definitions "available"; user-initiated + definitions "essential" (instructions stressed importance); mixed-initiative + available; mixed-initiative + essential
- **Key findings:**
  - Respondents with any clarification available answered more accurately on complicated scenarios than those with none: **67.5%** (user-initiated) and **66.4%** (mixed-initiative) vs. **40.9%** (no clarification)
  - Telling respondents clarification was "essential" improved accuracy further: ~78–83% vs. ~49–55%
  - Mixed-initiative and user-initiated clarification produced similar accuracy
  - Clarification had no effect on straightforward scenarios (~98–100% in all conditions)

## Experiment 2

- **N:** Not specified in extracted text (lab study)
- **Key addition:** Tested whether tailoring system-initiated clarification to individual respondent characteristics (specifically age — older respondents benefited more) outperformed a generic model of when all respondents need clarification
- **Finding:** Tailored clarification (based on respondent age) was more effective than generic clarification

## Additional findings

- Clarification increased response times, but respondents preferred having clarification available
- Respondents who obtained information (web search context) clicked for clarification almost twice as often as those providing information (survey context) — consistent with the idea that survey respondents are less motivated to clarify

## Key theoretical contribution

Frames web survey clarification as a *dialogue*: standard surveys embody a "message model" assuming questions are understood as written; a "collaborative model" recognizes that grounding mutual understanding requires back-and-forth. Mixed-initiative clarification (where the system can also initiate) implements this collaborative model.

## Relevance to tooltip usage

Extends the user-initiated tooltip literature by introducing mixed-initiative (system-proactive) clarification. Shows that accuracy improves when clarification is available regardless of who initiates it, but that respondents often don't initiate it even when they need it — motivating system-initiated approaches.
