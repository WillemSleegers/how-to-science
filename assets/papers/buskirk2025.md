## Scope

189 papers, January 2019–February 2025. Organises applications into pre-collection (instrument development, pretesting), data collection (silicon sampling, AI interviewing), and post-collection (coding, classification, imputation).

## Key findings

- Automated text classification of open-ended responses is the largest single application category (~48% of post-collection papers).
- Instrument development (item generation, translation, pretesting) is described as "well explored."
- Silicon sampling is the dominant data-collection application. The review flags representational accuracy and construct validity as open problems, citing Bisbee et al. (2024) and Mellon et al. (2024) as cautionary.
- Live interviewing and cross-lingual adaptation are under-investigated.
- Generalisability across populations, languages, and topics is essentially unestablished — few studies test more than one context.

## Note

This is a 6-page work-in-progress conference paper. The full systematic coding of success conditions, LLM used, and populations covered is not yet complete. Substantive conclusions are illustrative rather than meta-analytically derived. Useful as a reference citation and for situating the field, not as a source of specific empirical claims.
